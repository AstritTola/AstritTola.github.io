Research Overview

My research develops topology-aware machine learning methods that combine ideas from algebraic topology, geometry, and deep learning. I am especially interested in problems where graph structure and high-order interactions matter — molecules, biological networks, and cybersecurity knowledge graphs.

Three guiding questions drive the work:

  • How can topology serve as an inductive bias for graph neural networks and transformers?
  • What are the right topological summaries for structured scientific data (molecules, pathways, KGs)?
  • How can we scale persistence-based methods to real-world AI systems?
