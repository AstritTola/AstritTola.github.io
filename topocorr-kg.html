TopoCorr-KG

Topology-Aware Retrieval and Correlation Across Disparate Cybersecurity Knowledge Graphs

A Florida State University research project funded by the ReliaQuest Innovation Challenge, developing AI-driven methods for retrieving and correlating information across separate cybersecurity knowledge graphs — with the goal of improving cyber threat analysis and decision-making.

Project Overview

Modern cyber threat analysis depends on knowledge graphs (KGs) — structured representations of vulnerabilities, attacks, actors, tools, and infrastructure. In practice, these KGs are fragmented across organizations and standards (e.g., MITRE ATT&CK, CVE/NVD, internal SOC graphs), making it difficult to correlate signals and form a unified picture of an evolving threat.

TopoCorr-KG develops a new class of topology-aware retrieval and correlation methods that exploit the higher-order structure of knowledge graphs — going beyond pairwise edges to capture motifs, cycles, and persistent topological features that survive across disparate KGs. We combine ideas from:

  • Topological Data Analysis — persistent homology and Euler characteristic curves as structural fingerprints.
  • Graph Machine Learning — graph neural networks and transformer-based KG embeddings.
  • Retrieval-Augmented AI — using topological signatures to guide retrieval across heterogeneous graphs.

Research Aims

Aim 1 — Topological Signatures for KGs

Develop scalable topological descriptors that compactly summarize cybersecurity knowledge graphs and remain stable under noisy, evolving data.

Aim 2 — Cross-KG Retrieval

Design retrieval methods that align disparate KGs using topology-aware similarity, surfacing related entities, attack patterns, and vulnerabilities across sources.

Aim 3 — Threat Correlation & Analyst Support

Build correlation pipelines that combine topological retrieval with LLM-based reasoning to support cyber analysts in real-world threat investigation workflows.
